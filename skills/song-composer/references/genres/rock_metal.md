# Rock & Metal Structure

When generating J-Rock, Metal, Hard Rock, Heavy Metal, Core genres, use these specific meta-tags inside the `<music>` block to trigger the right musical nuances. 

**Ensure you follow the [Core Rules](../core_rules.md) regarding limits and base structure.**

## Typical Structural Tags

Rock and metal thrive on intensity, solos, and dynamic shifts.

- **`[guitar solo]`** or **`[bass solo]`** or **`[drums solo]`**: Place before or after a bridge to signify instrumental mastery.
- **`[breakdown]`**: Replace or add to a bridge to signify a heavy, palm-muted rhythmic section.
- **`[scream]`**, **`[growl]`**, **`[guttural]`**: Use on specific powerful lines or stanzas.
- **`[heavy riff]`**: Good to use after the intro or for connecting verses to choruses.

## Advanced Usage

Combine tags to guide the dynamic flow:

```
[bridge | breakdown | dropped tuning | slow halftime]
[Vocals: guttural scream]
{heavy lyrics here}

[guitar solo | shredding | melodic | fast]

[Final Chorus | double kick drums | stadium chant | group shouting]
{lyrics here}
```

## `<styles>` Guidance

When writing the `<styles>` block (max 1000 chars), include descriptors like: 
`Distorted Electric Guitar, Palm Muted Riffs, Melodic Lead Guitar, Driving Bass, Punchy Live Drums, Dynamic Builds, Group Shout, Stadium Chant, Full Band Explosion` as appropriate for the sub-genre requested.
